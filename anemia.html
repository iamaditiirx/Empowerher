Anemia

One of the most common signs of anemia is persistent fatigue and a general feeling of weakness. This occurs due to the decreased ability of the blood to carry oxygen to body tissues.

Risk Factors and Causes

Iron Deficiency: This is the most common cause of anemia globally. It occurs when the body lacks sufficient iron to produce hemoglobin. This can be due to poor diet, absorption issues, or excessive blood loss (such as during heavy menstruation).

Chronic Inflammation: Conditions like autoimmune disorders and chronic infections can lead to anemia by affecting the body's production of red blood cells.

Vitamin Deficiencies: Anemia can also result from deficiencies in other vital nutrients like vitamin B12 and folic acid. These nutrients are crucial for red blood cell production.

Precautions and Early Detection

Balanced Diet: Incorporate iron-rich foods like lean meats, fish, beans, and leafy greens into your diet. Vitamin C-rich foods like citrus fruits can enhance iron absorption.

Supplements: In cases of known deficiencies, consider taking iron, vitamin B12, or folic acid supplements under the guidance of a healthcare provider.

Manage Chronic Conditions: For individuals with chronic diseases, managing the underlying condition is essential in preventing anemia.
