Gestational infections

Persistent fever and chills can indicate an underlying infection. It's crucial to seek medical attention if you experience these symptoms.Unusual changes in vaginal discharge, such as increased volume, unusual color, or a foul odor, may be indicative of an infection.

Risk Factors and Causes

Bacterial Infections: Bacterial infections like urinary tract infections (UTIs), bacterial vaginosis, and group B streptococcus (GBS) can be contracted during pregnancy.

Viral Infections: Viruses such as cytomegalovirus (CMV), herpes simplex virus (HSV), and Zika virus can be transmitted to the fetus if the mother becomes infected.

Parasitic Infections: Parasitic infections like toxoplasmosis can pose risks to both the mother and the developing fetus.

Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs): Infections like syphilis, gonorrhea, and chlamydia can be transmitted from mother to fetus during pregnancy or childbirth.

Precautions and Early Detection

Prenatal Care: Regular prenatal check-ups are crucial for early detection and treatment of infections. This allows healthcare providers to monitor the mother's health and the development of the fetus.

Hygiene Practices: Maintaining good personal hygiene, including regular handwashing, can help prevent infections.
