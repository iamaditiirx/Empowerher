Urinary Tract Infection

UTIs can result from poor personal hygiene practices, such as inadequate wiping or improper cleaning. Lack of access to clean water and sanitation facilities can also contribute.

Risk Factors and Causes

Bacteria: Examples include Chlamydia, Gonorrhea, and Syphilis. These infections are treated with antibiotics.

Viruses: STIs like Herpes, HIV, and HPV are caused by viruses. While some viral STIs can be managed with medications, they may not have a cure.

Parasites: Trichomoniasis, caused by a parasite, can lead to symptoms like itching and discharge.

Precautions and Early Detection

Safe Sexual Practices: Practicing safe sex, including consistent and correct condom use, significantly reduces the risk of STIs.

Regular Testing: Getting tested for STIs, especially if sexually active with multiple partners, is crucial for early detection and treatment.br>
Open Communication: Discussing sexual health with partners and healthcare providers can help identify risks and establish safe practices.

Screening During Pregnancy: Routine STI screening during pregnancy is essential to protect both the mother and the unborn child.
