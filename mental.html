Mental Health

Depression:
Symptoms: Persistent sadness, loss of interest or pleasure, changes in sleep or appetite, feelings of hopelessness, and difficulty concentrating.
Causes: Genetic predisposition, biochemical imbalances, life events (e.g., loss, trauma), and chronic stress.
Prevention: Regular exercise, balanced nutrition, social support, and seeking professional help.

Anxiety Disorders:
Symptoms: Excessive worry, restlessness, irritability, muscle tension, and panic attacks.
Causes: Genetics, brain chemistry, traumatic experiences, and chronic medical conditions.
Prevention: Stress management techniques (e.g., mindfulness, deep breathing), regular physical activity, and therapy.

Bipolar Disorder:
Symptoms: Periods of extreme highs (mania) and lows (depression), changes in energy levels, impulsivity, and disrupted sleep patterns.
Causes: Genetic factors, imbalances in neurotransmitters, and environmental triggers.
Prevention: Medication management, psychoeducation, and establishing stable routines.
