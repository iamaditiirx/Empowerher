STIs

Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs) are a group of diseases that spread primarily through sexual contact. They can affect people of all ages, genders, and sexual orientations. It's crucial to be informed about STIs, their symptoms, causes, and most importantly, how to prevent them.

Risk Factors and Causes

STIs are caused by bacteria, viruses, or parasites. They are transmitted through unprotected sexual contact, which includes vaginal, anal, or oral sex. Additionally, some STIs can be transmitted from mother to child during childbirth or breastfeeding. Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs): Infections like syphilis, gonorrhea, and chlamydia can be transmitted from mother to fetus during pregnancy or childbirth.

Precautions and Early Detection

bstinence: The most effective way to prevent STIs is to abstain from sexual activity. This includes vaginal, anal, and oral sex..

Use of Condoms: Consistent and correct use of latex or polyurethane condoms during sexual activity can significantly reduce the risk of STIs.
